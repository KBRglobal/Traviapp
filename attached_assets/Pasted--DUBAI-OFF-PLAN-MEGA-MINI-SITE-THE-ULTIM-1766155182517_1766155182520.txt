**1. â–¶ What is off-plan property in Dubai and how does it work?**

Off-plan property refers to real estate units sold before or during construction, allowing buyers to purchase at pre-completion prices typically 15-25% below market value. In Dubai, off-plan developments are strictly regulated by Dubai Land Department (DLD) and Real Estate Regulatory Agency (RERA), with all payments held in escrow accounts and released only upon verified construction milestones.

**How the Process Works:**
1. Developer launches project and obtains DLD approval
2. Buyers sign Sales & Purchase Agreement (SPA)
3. Initial deposit (10-20%) goes to DLD-regulated escrow account
4. Remaining payments tied to construction milestones (foundation, structure, completion)
5. Independent engineers verify each milestone before escrow releases funds
6. Upon completion, buyer receives keys and Title Deed

**December 2025 Context:**
According to Property Finder, November 2024 saw 8,548 off-plan transactions valued at AED 12.4 billion, with international buyers representing 73% of purchasers. The market shows sustained momentum driven by three mega-developments: Dubai Creek Harbour, MBR City, and Expo City Dubai expansion.

**Key Regulations:**
- **Escrow Protection:** Law No. 8 of 2007 mandates all payments go to escrow
- **Milestone Verification:** Independent engineers must certify progress
- **RERA Licensing:** Only licensed developers can sell off-plan
- **Buyer Rights:** Full refund from escrow if project canceled

**Why It's Safe:**
Dubai's off-plan system is rated #1 globally for buyer protection (JLL Report 2024). The escrow system eliminates traditional pre-construction risksâ€”even if a developer faces bankruptcy, your funds remain protected in escrow and are refunded within 90 days.

**Typical Timeline:**
